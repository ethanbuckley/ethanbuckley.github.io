Technical note · Down Syndrome Education International

Modelling vocabulary growth: the statistics & computation

A Bayesian hierarchical model family that turns sparse, multi-study checklist counts into full probability distributions over children's vocabulary trajectories, plus the pipeline that fits, checks and reproduces it.

Bayesian hierarchical · PyMC + nutpie (NUTS) · Beta-Binomial + HSGP · 15 models (VG01–VG16)

The estimation problem

The data are bounded word counts (0–810) pooled from eleven studies, sparse and repeated on the same children. Three features drive the design: counts are over-dispersed (more between-child spread than a Binomial allows); studies differ in composition and instrument; and speaking is conditional on understanding. Every quantity is estimated as a posterior distribution, so results carry explicit uncertainty rather than a single point.

Core model

yi ~ BetaBinomial(N = 810,  mean = pi,  concentration = κ(ai))   // over-dispersed counts, age-varying dispersion
pU(a) = σ(fU(a))   // words understood
q(a) = σ(h(a)) = P(speak | understand)   // production ratio
pS(a) = pU(a) · q(a)   ⟹  pS ≤ pU by construction
f(a) = trend(a) + g(a),  g ~ HSGP   // smooth nonparametric mean; Beta anchor priors at set ages
f•(zi)  +=  δstudys(i) + δsubjj(i),  δ = τ·z   // non-centred random intercepts

The production ratio q is the model's pivot: the share of understood words a child also says. A Gaussian process (approximated with a scalable HSGP basis) carries the trajectory shape; study- and child-level random intercepts separate the population curve from who was sampled and from repeated measures.

The model family

  • SingleOne outcome: understood or spoken alone, the shared building block. VG01–04, 11–12
  • JointUnderstood + spoken via the q-decomposition, with study and subject random effects. VG05, 07–10, 13
  • SigningAdds a sign ratio r; VG15 adds a Plackett copula ψ + Dirichlet-Multinomial on the sign×speech cross-tab to identify their overlap. VG14–15
  • LongitudinalWithin-child cross-lag: does earlier comprehension predict later production? VG16
  • DS vs TDPer-draw differences of disjoint posteriors: because the populations share no children, the contrast is exact, with no joint model needed.

Inference & pipeline

  • PyMC
  • nutpie (Rust NUTS)
  • HSGP
  • ArviZ
  • DuckDB
  • Quarto
  • PSIS-LOO
  • leave-one-study-out CV

Fitting runs full-posterior MCMC (NUTS via nutpie), screened with prior- and posterior-predictive checks and standard diagnostics: R̂, effective sample size, divergences. Models are compared with PSIS-LOO and leave-one-study-out cross-validation. Studies are harmonised in a DuckDB view onto the common 800-item scale, and the whole run (data → posteriors → figures → report in Quarto) is reproducible at three sampling tiers (dev / test / rep), so every result regenerates from raw data as the models improve.

Workflow & rigour. Built as an iterative Bayesian workflow: interpretable anchor priors informed by expectation and independent Wordbank norms, explicit identification arguments, and caveats where they matter (e.g. short-panel bias makes the naïve within-child lag misleading, so VG16 headlines a bias-robust baseline). A methodology and code audit independently verified the statistical core and fixed several issues (a comprehension-proxy data leak, a GP-boundary mis-sizing, young-age prior calibration, and per-form ceiling validation) before the reporting-quality run.

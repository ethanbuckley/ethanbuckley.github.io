A spatial epidemic on a grid — and how it was checked against the maths

A stochastic 2D cellular automaton of epidemic spread across a spatially heterogeneous population, extended individually from a UCL group project. Its local update rules are shown to reduce to the classical well-mixed SEIQR ODE in the mean-field limit — so the "checked against an analytical model" claim is derived, not asserted, and the spatial model's departure from that reference becomes interpretable.

Stochastic spatial CA · NumPy + scipy.convolve2d · validated vs the analytical SEIQR ODE · reproducible & CI-tested · Educational project

The model

A 50×50 grid of cells, each in one of five states — Susceptible, Exposed, Infected, Quarantined, Recovered. A fixed density map sets three concentric exposure zones: a dense centre, a suburban ring, and sparse outskirts. Each timestep, a susceptible cell with at least one infected Moore neighbour is exposed with its zone's probability; the exposed become infected, the infected either quarantine or recover on a single random draw, and the quarantined recover. The one-cell border is held permanently susceptible and excluded from the counts, so the participating population is the 48×48 = 2,304-cell interior.

Validation against the ODE

per-step probability → continuous rate:   ρ = −ln(1 − p)   // not ρ ≈ p, which is 28% off at p_infect = 0.5
mean-field force of infection:   λ(i) = −ln(1 − pexpose·(1 − (1 − i)8))
low-prevalence slope β = 8·pexpose   ⟹   R₀ = β / (γ_Q + γ_R) ≈ 15 at pexpose = 0.30
I-exit is one draw: split I→Q / I→R by combined hazard ρtot = −ln(1 − p_Q − p_Ri) in ratio p_Q : p_Ri

The honest claim is a reduction, not a coincidence: the CA's local Moore-neighbour rule, averaged over a well-mixed population, is the classical SEIQR ODE with these rates. Matching the ODE therefore tests that the stochastic rules were implemented correctly — while the spatial model's departure from it is then interpretable as structure, not as a bug.

What the comparison shows

Driven by the global infected fraction instead of local neighbours (the well-mixed limit), the CA ensemble reproduces the exact discrete recursion and the continuous ODE to about 2% of peak (RMSE), with an essentially identical final attack rate, at R₀ ≈ 15. The standard local (spatial) CA departs — a peak roughly 3.5× lower and about 50 steps later. That gap is the genuine effect of spatial structure and local susceptible depletion, which is the reason to use a cellular automaton in the first place, not a validation failure. Both curves use the same 2,304-cell interior, so the CA and the ODE share a denominator.

Key findings

  • Density gradientLower, earlier peak on the heterogeneous grid than a uniform one — the sparse outskirts act as a natural brake on transmission.
  • WaveInfection spreads outward in a measurable wave: the dense centre peaks first and highest, the middle ring follows, the outskirts last.
  • Targeted lockdownLocking down only the 289-cell centre (12% of the grid) gives suppression comparable to a whole-grid lockdown, at a fraction of the cost.
  • Targeted vaccination200 doses concentrated in the centre dramatically outperform the same 200 spread uniformly.
  • CombinedCentre vaccination + centre-only lockdown is the best outcome for the fewest resources.

How it is built

The per-cell Python loops of the original group code were replaced with a single scipy.signal.convolve2d for Moore-neighbour counting and NumPy boolean masking for the transitions — about 40× faster per run. Every scenario is regenerated from fixed seeds, and a regression test asserts the published curves stay bit-identical; a headless test checks the dashboard loads; both run in CI across Python 3.10–3.12. An interactive Streamlit dashboard drives the model live.
